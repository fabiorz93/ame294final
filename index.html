About Me

I am Fabio, a Brazilian Student currently enrolled at Arizona State University.

In 2011, I entered on the Federal University of ABC, in Santo Andre, Sao Paolo, Brazil, as a Bachelor in Science and Technology student, a major that give us some fairly background in the empirical, formal and social fields of science. Having this background, I decided to continue my studies in Computer Science, because of the many possibities that the knowledge in this area can offer. Programming can be a tool for creativity, can be useful on many different fields and offers endless possibilities to solve real-world problems.

In 2013, looking for new perspectives, I decided to apply for the Brazilian Scientific Mobility Program, which is a federal grant for outstanding students, giving them the possibility of spending a year studying in a foreign country. I choose the United States, and in 2014 I arrived in Arizona State University, where I am right now.

I have interest of working with Computer Science in areas where I can be creative, to develop apps, websites, softwares and solutions for everyday life, as you can see below.

Nice to meet you!

Games

A brief overview on the games I developed for the courses Game Development I and II, respectively.

Plot Twist

Plot Twist (Subtitle: ‘Cause Life Is Not Only Made By Choices) is a Platform/Decision-Based Game created as a tribute to the classical 8-bit platform game style. You play as Twister, a 15 years-old regular boy, which has to walk through a sort of situations (represented by mini-games) that leads him to a sort of unforeseen, dangerous events and enemies.

Although is a platform-game, the decisions you take while playing the game leads you to different paths, which can take you to different endings. Overall, “Plot Twist” is a play over usual situations involving a 15-years-old once-regular boy with absurd outcomes, and an attempt of using a traditional game-style to tell a non-linear story.

  • Platform Used: Game Maker
  • Release Date: December 2014

Framing

In “Framing”, you are a civil construction worker stuck at the top of a falling steel frame structure, and your objective is to find your way down to the ground without falling or getting hurt by the falling pieces of the structure.

This game is a three-dimensional approach of a “Snake & Ladders” concept, considering its characteristic of offering shortcuts that helps the player to go down, or obstacles that forces the player to return to a previous floor, depending on the way he chooses to go.

At the end of the game, a message is displayed, discussing the poor work conditions in what millions of workers around the world are exposed every day.

  • Platform Used: Unity3D
  • Release Date: May 7th, 2015 (Upcoming)
